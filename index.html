Cost per month vs speed

Drag to zoom into the dense cluster. Hover any point — stacked plans expand to show each name and its validity. Beside each plan's monthly cost, Δ is how much more it costs per month than the cheapest plan offering at least its speed ("at least", not "exactly"; faster plans count too!)

Value frontieriConnects the best deal at every speed — the cheapest plan you can buy at each speed level.Each step up is tagged with what the extra speed costs, $\frac{\Delta\,\text{cost/mo}}{\Delta\,\text{Mbps}}$. The knee is the plan after which each extra Mbps suddenly gets far pricier — picked as the largest jump in that step-cost from one tier to the next.
Best-value lineiThe single plan that gives the most speed per rupee; the line runs from zero straight through it.It minimises the rate $\frac{\text{cost/mo}}{\text{Mbps}}$. Any plan below the line beats it, and the flatter the line, the cheaper each Mbps.
Least-cost lineiThe cheapest plan on the board, whatever its speed — the least you can spend just to get online.A flat line at $\min(\text{cost/mo})$, ignoring speed entirely.
OLS fitiThe overall trend — on average, how much more you pay as speed rises.A least-squares line $\text{cost}\approx m\cdot\text{speed}+c$. The slope $m$ is the typical ₹ per Mbps; $R^2$ is how tightly the plans hug that trend.
↺Reset scaleiZoomed in too far? Snaps the chart back to its full view, undoing any drag-zoom on either axis.
↘ Heteroscedastic: cost spread is tight near the origin and fans out at high speed — billing term, not speed alone, drives the divergence.
